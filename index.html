Maryam Amirizaniani

Ph.D student at University of Washington

I am a third-year Ph.D. student at the Information School, University of Washington, where I am fortunate to be advised by Prof. Chirag Shah. Previously, I completed my Master’s degree at Toronto Metropolitan University under the supervision of Prof. Farid Shirazi.

My research centers on Large Foundation Models, with a particular focus on the following areas:

  • Personalization: Enabling browsing models to make context-aware and personalized decisions by leveraging users’ prior behavior and input patterns.
  • Reliability: Investigating how information retrieval techniques can support more reliable and grounded model outputs, enhancing both trustworthiness and explainability in decision-making contexts.
  • Alignment: Investigating methods to align model outputs more effectively with human intent and values, ensuring responses are helpful, safe, and grounded in human expectations.
